## What is KarmaLinks?

[KarmaLinks](https://karmalinks.io/) is a backlink exchange club built specifically for B2B SaaS companies. Members earn "karma" points equal to their Domain Rating by giving backlinks, then spend points to receive backlinks, matched by AI on niche, domain authority, and content relevance. It uses ABC triangular exchanges (A links to B, B links to C, C links to A) instead of direct reciprocal links — a structure generally considered safer under Google's guidance on [excessive link exchanges](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies) than simple "you link to me, I'll link to you" swaps. Only do-follow links are accepted, and joining requires a minimum Domain Rating of 15.

## Where KarmaLinks still wins

KarmaLinks is closest in spirit to Exchange Backlinks and genuinely free at the entry tier. If your site clears the DR 15 bar and you specifically want an automated points marketplace with ABC-style rotation rather than a direct conversation with a partner site, it's worth trying alongside — it's a fit for SaaS teams comfortable trading some visibility for automation.

## Why teams switch to Exchange Backlinks

No DR minimum to join — smaller or newer B2B sites are still welcome, and you're not limited to SaaS. [Submit your site](/submit) and instead of spending points into a triangular exchange with sites you can't fully vet upfront, you get matched with one specific site in your niche, see who they are, and agree on the placement directly before anything goes live. For other points- or credit-based exchanges, see [RankChase](/alternatives/rankchase) and [Authority Exchange](/alternatives/authority-exchange).

## Frequently asked questions

### Is KarmaLinks free?

Yes, at the entry tier — pricing starts at $0, and you begin exchanging backlinks immediately once you meet the DR 15 minimum. That's a real difference from most of the other paid tools on this list.

### Do I need a minimum Domain Rating to join KarmaLinks?

Yes, DR 15 or higher. Sites below that threshold aren't eligible, which rules out newer or smaller B2B sites that Exchange Backlinks doesn't gate on DR at all.

### Is KarmaLinks' ABC exchange safer for SEO than a direct link swap?

Triangular (ABC) exchanges avoid the direct reciprocal pattern ("we both link to each other") that Google's link spam guidance flags most explicitly, so they carry somewhat less structural risk than a two-way swap — though scale and automation still matter.

### What's the best KarmaLinks alternative if my site doesn't meet the DR requirement?

Exchange Backlinks has no DR minimum, so it's the more accessible option for newer sites still building authority.
