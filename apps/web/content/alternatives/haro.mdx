## What is HARO?

HARO (Help a Reporter Out) matches journalists who need sources with people
willing to be quoted, delivered as email digests. Cision shut down the
original HARO and tried a paid "Connectively" relaunch in 2024, which users
rejected; Featured.com then acquired the brand and relaunched HARO free in
April 2025 — three digests a day, 40–100 requests each.

## HARO vs Exchange Backlinks

| | HARO | Exchange Backlinks |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What's exchanged | Quotes for journalists' articles | Backlinks, directly |
| Backlink outcome | Not guaranteed — depends on the journalist crediting you | Guaranteed by the match |
| Price | Free | Free |
| Time cost | Scanning + pitching, 15–45 min per digest, 3x/day | One weekly email to read and reply to |
| Niche | General press, any beat | B2B niches only |

## Where HARO still wins

A quote picked up by a real publication carries a kind of authority a
reciprocal exchange never will — press mentions, brand exposure, and
sometimes a much stronger link than anything you'd negotiate directly. If
PR reach is the goal, HARO still does something Exchange Backlinks doesn't.

## Why teams switch to Exchange Backlinks

HARO is free in dollars but expensive in time — three digests a day, most
requests irrelevant, no guarantee your pitch gets used or linked. Exchange
Backlinks is one email a week, matched specifically to your niche, and the
backlink is the actual deal — not a maybe.
