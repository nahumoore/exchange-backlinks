## What is HARO?

HARO (Help a Reporter Out) matches journalists who need sources with people willing to be quoted, delivered as email digests. Cision shut down the original HARO and tried a paid "Connectively" relaunch in 2024, which users rejected; [Featured.com](https://www.featured.com/) then acquired the brand and relaunched HARO free in April 2025 — three digests a day, 40–100 requests each, back to the original format people used it for.

## Where HARO still wins

A quote picked up by a real publication carries a kind of authority a reciprocal exchange never will — press mentions, brand exposure, and sometimes a much stronger link than anything you'd negotiate directly. If PR reach is the goal, HARO still does something Exchange Backlinks doesn't, and it's worth running alongside a link exchange rather than instead of one.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is HARO still free?

Yes. After Cision's paid "Connectively" relaunch was rejected by users, Featured.com acquired HARO and brought back the free, email-digest format in April 2025.

### How many HARO requests come through per day?

Three digests a day, each with roughly 40–100 requests across every industry and beat — most of which won't be relevant to a B2B niche site.

### Does answering a HARO request guarantee a backlink?

No. Whether the journalist credits you — and links to you — is entirely their call, and plenty of used quotes go uncredited or unlinked.
